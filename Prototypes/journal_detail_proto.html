Heavy Winter Rain

The renovation has reached its most difficult stage—the skeletal transition. Today, the rain was incessant, a constant rhythmic drumming against the tarp. It matches the state of the house: exposed, vulnerable, and loudly quiet.

We spent the morning debating the merits of raw versus sealed oak. There is a specific kind of beauty in the unrefined that I fear losing once the finishing coats are applied. It’s a metaphor, I suppose, for this entire year. Everything is in mid-process, lacking the polish of completion but vibrating with the potential of what it might become.

"The house feels like a living thing right now, breathing through the gaps in the floorboards. We are just guests in its transformation."

Mood remains low. 3/10 feels generous. It’s hard to find the center of a home when the center is currently a pile of drywall and sawdust. Yet, seeing the way the light hits the new vaulted ceiling at 4 PM—even in the grey rain—reminds me why we started this.
