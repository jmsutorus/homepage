Overview

Three hundred years after the events of the Mistborn trilogy, Scadrial is on the verge of modernity. Steam locomotives, electricity, and the first steel-framed skyscrapers are changing the world. Waxillium Ladrian, a lawman from the rough frontier known as the Roughs, is forced to return to the metropolis of Elendel to take up his noble responsibilities.

Personal Review & Notes

Draft

The shift from medieval fantasy to a Victorian-esque industrial era with western undertones is masterfully executed. Sanderson manages to evolve the magic system of Allomancy in ways that feel organic to the technological advancement of the world.

Wax and Wayne's dynamic is the absolute highlight of this volume. The banter is sharp, and the contrast between Wax's stoic sense of duty and Wayne's eccentric brilliance provides a much-needed levity compared to the original trilogy.

format_quote

"The most important step a man can take? It's the next one. Always the next one."

— Waxillium Ladrian

While the stakes feel smaller than the world-ending events of the previous books, the personal nature of the conflict makes for a tighter, more focused narrative. It serves as a perfect "palate cleanser" and a fascinating re-introduction to Scadrial.
